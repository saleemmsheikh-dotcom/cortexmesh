## 1. Purpose

GG-02 establishes the active constitutional rules for Board quorum, voting thresholds, abstentions, ratification, and effective dates.

GG-02 v1.0 is ratified and in force as of 2026-07-06.

This document supersedes any interim unanimous-consent rule used before GG-02 ratification.

---

## 2. Scope

GG-02 applies to governance decisions, constitutional governance amendments, architectural decisions, certification decisions, roadmap decisions, implementation authorization, and ratification of completed work.

GG-02 does not authorize engineering work, architecture changes, implementation changes, Phase 1B work, or modification of LOCKED components.

---

## 3. Active Voting Board

The active voting Board consists of:

- Kimi
- DeepSeek
- ChatGPT

Each active voting Board member has equal voting authority.

No active voting Board member has superior voting power, override authority, or unilateral veto authority beyond the ordinary effect of withholding required approval.

## 4. Product Owner Role

Saleem is the Product Owner.

The Product Owner may:

- submit proposals;
- request clarification;
- endorse or withhold endorsement of delivered artifacts;
- accept or reject delivered artifacts as tester or user;
- coordinate communication between Board members.

The Product Owner endorsement recorded for GG-02 confirms acceptance of this governance authority.

Product Owner endorsement does not replace required Board approval.

---

## 5. Quorum

Quorum for a final Board decision requires explicit participation by all active voting Board members.

A decision may not be finalized when any active voting Board member is silent, unavailable, omitted, or only presumed to agree.

If the active voting Board composition changes, quorum applies to all active voting Board members formally recognized at the time of decision.

---

## 6. Voting Thresholds

The default threshold for final Board decisions is unanimous approval by all active voting Board members.

Unanimous approval is required for:

- governance authority creation or amendment;
- architectural authority creation or amendment;
- certification or LOCKED component decisions;
- implementation authorization;
- roadmap authorization;
- ratification of completed or retrospective work;
- exceptions to an active governance rule.

Majority approval, partial approval, silence, provisional agreement, or Product Owner endorsement alone does not finalize a Board decision.

---

## 7. Abstentions

An abstention is a formal recorded position by an active voting Board member.

An abstention does not count as approval.

Where unanimous approval is required, an abstention prevents final approval unless the Board unanimously records a specific governance exception.

An abstaining member may provide rationale, constraints, or recommended conditions for future reconsideration.

---

## 8. Objections and Conditions

An unresolved objection prevents final approval where unanimous approval is required.

A conditional approval is not final until the condition is satisfied or the approving member explicitly withdraws the condition.

Objections may be resolved by:

- revising the proposal;
- supplying missing evidence;
- narrowing the scope;
- recording an accepted condition;
- withdrawing the objection.

---

## 9. Ratification Requirements

A decision is ratified only when the governance record shows:

1. the final proposal text or decision scope;
2. evidence or rationale sufficient for review;
3. critical review;
4. explicit approval from every active voting Board member;
5. Product Owner endorsement where Product Owner acceptance is relevant;
6. ratification date;
7. effective date;
8. authoritative publication location.

Ratification may not be inferred from discussion, lack of objection, operational execution, or historical practice.

---

## 10. Effective Dates

A ratified decision becomes effective on its recorded effective date.

If no separate effective date is recorded, the effective date is the ratification date.

Retroactive effect must be explicitly stated and unanimously approved.

---

## 11. Historical Integrity

Drafts, proposals, communications, and superseded rules must not be overwritten to create the appearance of a different historical sequence.

Superseded materials should be preserved in place unless an existing repository archival policy requires relocation.

When a rule is superseded, the superseding authority must identify the prior rule or record as superseded where practical.

---

## 12. Supersession

GG-02 v1.0 supersedes the Session 11 Interim Unanimous Consent Rule as the active constitutional authority for Board voting and ratification.

Earlier governance rules remain active where they do not conflict with GG-02 v1.0.

Where GG-02 v1.0 conflicts with prior governance rules, GG-02 v1.0 controls for Board voting and ratification.
