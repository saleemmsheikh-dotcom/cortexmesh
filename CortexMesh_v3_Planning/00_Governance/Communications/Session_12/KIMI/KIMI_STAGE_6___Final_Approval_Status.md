> **EXECUTIVE SUMMARY**
>
> Following a structured five-stage evaluation, the Expert Panel unanimously concludes that CortexMesh has a genuine architectural planning gap but that evidence supports only a preventive architectural planning initiative, not implementation authority. The Panel recommends that the Board consider authorising a bounded Architecture Study with advisory status only.
>
> ---
>
> **1. PROBLEM DEFINITION**
>
> CortexMesh has successfully completed Phases 1B, 2A, 2C, and 3A but lacks a formally defined engineering framework governing long-term architectural evolution, including criteria for future capability selection, architectural evolution principles, integration boundaries, transition pathways, and governance alignment for capability expansion. This gap is preventive, not corrective.
>
> **2. SCOPE DEFINITION**
>
> Programme Alpha is classified as an **Architecture Study** with advisory authority only. It possesses no implementation authority, no governance amendment authority, and no roadmap approval authority.
>
> **3. EVIDENCE ASSESSMENT**
>
> Evidence supports the existence of the architectural planning gap (absence of evolution framework, completed phases without integration plan, evolution-relevant technical debt, War Room strategic gaps). Evidence does not support implementation authority (completed phases were successful, no documented architectural failure). The recommendation is preventive.
>
> **4. STRATEGIC ALTERNATIVES**
>
> | Rank | Alternative | Position |
> |------|-------------|----------|
> | 1 | Architecture Study | **Recommended** |
> | 2 | Architecture Study → TDR enhancements | Future conditional |
> | 3 | Architecture Study → SGC-001 refinement | Future conditional |
> | 4 | Hybrid after Architecture Study | Deferred |
> | 5 | Do Nothing | Rejected |
>
> **5. ENGINEERING RECOMMENDATION**
>
> 1. Authorise an Architecture Study as the proportionate response to the identified architectural planning gap.
> 2. Limit the Study to advisory authority only.
> 3. Require the Study to produce a bounded engineering blueprint (architectural evolution principles, capability selection criteria, integration boundaries, transition pathways, measurable success criteria, evidence thresholds for future implementation proposals).
> 4. Require the Study to explicitly evaluate, rather than presume: targeted Technical Debt Register enhancements, targeted SGC-001 refinements, roadmap evolution, future strategic engineering programme, and no further action.
> 5. Require any implementation proposal arising from the Study to return through GG-02 unanimous Board approval.
>
> **6. GOVERNANCE COMPLIANCE**
>
> The recommendation preserves constitutional framework: implementation authority withheld, governance amendment authority not exercised, existing GG-02 Board authority preserved, future implementation contingent upon separate Board approval.
>
> **7. RISK ACKNOWLEDGEMENT**
>
> The Architecture Study mitigates War Room risks (WR-R001 through WR-R006) while avoiding premature implementation authority. Future Board review required before any implementation.
>
> **8. TECHNICAL DEBT ACKNOWLEDGEMENT**
>
> The Architecture Study must acknowledge all active Technical Debt items recorded in the Technical Debt Register v1.0 (TD-001 through TD-015): orchestrator randomness (TD-001), memory integrity (TD-002), Merkle anchoring (TD-003), Docker isolation (TD-004), timeout/zombie processes (TD-005), legal licence (TD-006), repository hygiene (TD-007), research terminology consistency (TD-009), independent reproduction (TD-010), comparative H1 evaluation (TD-011), adversarial corpus expansion (TD-012), provider-neutral envelope evolution (TD-013), capability-aware routing (TD-014), and register maintenance (TD-015). The Study must not supersede or replace the Register.
>
> **9. HISTORICAL INTEGRITY**
>
> Completed phases remain authoritative. Governance history unchanged. Historical Board decisions preserved. The Architecture Study is forward-looking only.
>
> **10. RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS**
