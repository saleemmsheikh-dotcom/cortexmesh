## Rationale

Phase 2A achieved provider neutrality, manager ownership, shared provider contracts, repeatable provider certification, and validated extension without extracting shared adapter utilities.

Subsequent Foundation work established the isolated Reference Orchestration Engine, permanent validation methodology, certified replay assets, and research infrastructure. It introduced no evidence that the limited HTTP transport, elapsed-time, or endpoint-validation duplication now causes measurable defects, maintenance cost, provider coupling, or extension failure.

The original engineering conclusion therefore remains valid: extraction without measurable benefit would risk premature coupling between materially different provider request, response, health, completion, and diagnostic semantics.

## Reassessment Triggers

Future reassessment remains evidence-driven and should occur only when at least one of the following is documented:

- a third production transport-backed provider is proposed;
- repeated transport duplication causes measurable defects;
- duplicated maintenance produces measurable delivery or change cost;
- provider certification evidence demonstrates a reusable contract boundary;
- a research or engineering assessment identifies a material, reproducible benefit from extraction.

Triggering reassessment does not authorize implementation. Any proposed change requires separate design, validation, boundary review, and applicable authorization.
