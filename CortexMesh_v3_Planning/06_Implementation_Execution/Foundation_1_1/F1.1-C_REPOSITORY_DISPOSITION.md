## Purpose

This review assigns a protection class and disposition to every item or item
group identified by the Phase 1 inventory. It is a decision record, not
authority to execute repository cleanup.

No file or directory may be moved, renamed, deleted, regenerated, or archived
on the strength of this document alone. Execution requires a separate reviewed
plan, a dedicated branch, pre-change verification, and explicit authorization.

## Protection classes

| Class | Meaning | Approval level |
| --- | --- | --- |
| P0 | Protected: research evidence, runtime, LOCKED, governance, validation, or authoritative records | Never move or alter without explicit scope-specific approval |
| P1 | Historical record or evidence-bearing artifact | Move only with provenance, hashes, references, and history preserved |
| P2 | Organizational asset | May be relocated after dependency and reference verification |
| P3 | Disposable generated or broken material | May be removed after verification that it is untracked, reproducible, or valueless |

Protection class does not establish authority. In particular, P3 means eligible
for removal after verification, not immediately disposable.

## Disposition vocabulary

| Disposition | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Retain | Keep in its current location and role. |
| Relocate | Preserve but move to an approved destination. |
| Archive | Preserve as historical material outside the active surface. |
| Remove | Delete after the required verification and authorization. |
| Defer | Make no structural decision until named evidence or authority exists. |

## Disposition summary

| Class | Governing treatment |
| --- | --- |
| P0 | Retain unless a separate architecture, research, validation, governance, legal, or LOCKED process explicitly authorizes change. |
| P1 | Preserve provenance and hashes; archive or relocate only through a recorded evidence-preserving operation. |
| P2 | Verify references and dependencies, then relocate in a reviewable organizational commit. |
| P3 | Verify untracked/reproducible/valueless status, then remove in a scoped cleanup operation. |

The decisions intentionally favor retention and deferral where provenance is
uncertain. Repository neatness does not outweigh evidence integrity.

## Preconditions for an execution branch

Before creating a cleanup branch:

1. accept this disposition record;
2. define exact source and destination paths for every Relocate or Archive
   action;
3. resolve or exclude all Deferred items;
4. record hashes for every P1 item in execution scope;
5. capture the clean baseline commit and worktree state;
6. enumerate runtime, LOCKED, governance, validation, and research exclusions;
7. define link, import, test, regression, and evidence-integrity checks;
8. define rollback as a normal Git reversal plus restoration of any external
   archive copy; and
9. authorize a narrowly scoped cleanup branch.

## Explicit non-decisions

This review does not:

- authorize cleanup execution;
- authorize a commit or branch;
- approve runtime or architectural restructuring;
- approve movement of P0 content;
- approve alteration of published evidence;
- resolve F1.1-B legal review;
- retrospectively create or redefine project phases; or
- permit history rewriting.
