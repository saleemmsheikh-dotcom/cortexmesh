# Architectural Mission

## What CortexMesh Is

CortexMesh is an orchestration architecture for multi-agent reasoning.

It coordinates solver agents, critic agents, scoring, authority selection, memory, governance, and external execution boundaries into one auditable decision process.

CortexMesh is designed to make agentic work inspectable, contestable, repeatable, and governed.

## What CortexMesh Is Not

CortexMesh is not a single model, chatbot, wrapper, benchmark script, or prompt collection.

It is not a replacement for human authority.

It is not an unchecked autonomy system.

It is not a policy document, certification claim, or implementation guarantee.

## Why CortexMesh Exists

CortexMesh exists to explore how complex AI work can be performed by governed systems of specialized agents rather than by a single opaque response path.

Its architectural purpose is to separate generation, criticism, scoring, authority, memory, and execution so that each part can be inspected and improved without collapsing the whole system into one hidden judgment.

The long-term mission is to build an AI coordination architecture where better decisions emerge from structured disagreement, explicit trust boundaries, recorded evidence, and accountable selection.
