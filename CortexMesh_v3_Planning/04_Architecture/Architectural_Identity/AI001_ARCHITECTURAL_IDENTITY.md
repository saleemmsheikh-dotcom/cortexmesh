# AI001 — Architectural Identity

## Classification

PROPOSED

## Core Statement

CortexMesh is a decision-governance shell around AI systems.

It is not primarily an AI system.

It is not primarily a model-ranking system.

It is not primarily a content-generation system.

It is not an autonomous decision-maker.

## Architectural Role

CortexMesh sits between:

Human decision authority

and

External AI agents or systems

Its role is to govern the decision process, not to replace the decision-maker.

## Responsibilities Accepted

CortexMesh accepts responsibility for:

- Explicit intent confirmation
- Draft-before-final lifecycle
- Auditability of governed process
- Separation of precedent from decision
- Human review checkpoints
- Governance of when AI outputs become eligible for delivery
- Preservation of procedural accountability

## Responsibilities Refused

CortexMesh refuses responsibility for:

- Generating substantive content
- Ranking agents or models
- Maintaining aggregate performance metrics
- Creating leaderboards
- Acting as arbiter between agent outputs
- Silently inferring intent
- Retaining rejected drafts
- Becoming a decision authority

## Architectural Identity Principle

CortexMesh shall be defined by both:

1. The responsibilities it accepts.
2. The responsibilities it explicitly refuses.

Comparative analysis with external systems exists to clarify these boundaries, not to establish superiority or copy mechanisms.

## Working Formula

CortexMesh does not ask:

"How can AI decide better?"

CortexMesh asks:

"How can decisions remain governable when AI participates?"

## Status

This document is a proposed architectural identity artifact.

It is not ratified policy.
