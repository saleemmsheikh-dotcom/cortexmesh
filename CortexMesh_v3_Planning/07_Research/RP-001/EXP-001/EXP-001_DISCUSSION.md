## Observed Characterization

The sealed engine produced invariant canonical results for all 24 cases across ten repetitions. Statuses, certified consensus classifications, synthesis structures, evidence identities, provenance, trace/correlation relationships, minority evidence, unresolved divergence, and registered diagnostics were stable.

This supports a narrow empirical statement: under the declared environment, corpus, harness, and simulated inputs, the Phase 2C reference engine is deterministic and replay reproducible for the EXP-001 study set.

It does not establish behavior outside this fixed corpus or environment.

## Evidence and Traceability

All 450 emitted evidence records contained the registered descriptive fields and resolvable record, step, trace, and correlation identifiers. Missing-output and rejected-input cases remained diagnosable without fabricating evidence.

The observed completeness is bounded to schema/characteristic presence. It does not assert that every possible real-world fact or claim is present.

## Consensus, Minority Evidence, and Divergence

All 24 certified categories matched. All three registered minority cases retained minority evidence, and all four material-divergence cases retained unresolved divergence in synthesis. These results characterize deterministic preservation, not correctness by vote or authority.

## Synthesis and Diagnostics

Synthesis artifacts were stable for all cases, and all registered diagnostic expectations were observed. Statement-level comparison identified four stable differences:

- compatible-alias cases `c02` and `c17` synthesize the canonical policy term rather than repeating every source alias;
- insufficient-evidence cases `c05` and `c09` do not promote a lone raw claim into a finding.

The raw claims remain in evidence records. The anomaly therefore concerns the intended meaning of corpus `statements` expectations: whether they require literal synthesis presentation or merely evidence preservation. EXP-001 does not resolve that semantic question and changes neither corpus nor engine.

## Latency

Observed overall median latency was 0.647353 ms and nearest-rank p95 was 0.931067 ms. Expected rejection paths were materially shorter than completed paths, and isolated higher observations raised the coefficient of variation. Latency was sufficiently recorded to form an environment-specific descriptive reference, but no performance threshold or optimization claim follows.

## Outcome Interpretation

**BASELINE CHARACTERIZED WITH ANOMALIES** is the appropriate registered outcome. The dataset, analysis, and anomalies are complete and reproducible. Matching stability results do not erase the four statement-expectation differences, and those differences do not erase the independent stability results.

The next scientific action, if approved, is review of the reproducibility package and anomaly classification. Engine or corpus remediation is outside EXP-001.
