## Execution Procedure

1. Record repository HEAD, registered tag resolutions, worktree state, clock, operating system, architecture, interpreter, and dependencies.
2. Validate the Replay Corpus v1.1 manifest, schema, certification, file ordering, and content hash.
3. Record the exact focused replay command and canonicalization/comparator versions without executing it before authorization.
4. Create an immutable experiment run identifier and output manifest.
5. Execute all 24 cases in manifest order for repetition 1; retain every output, diagnostic, timing, and exit state.
6. Execute repetitions 2-10 identically; do not discard warm-up, failure, or anomalous observations.
7. Compare every repetition with certified expectations.
8. Compare canonical artifacts in repetitions 2-10 with repetition 1.
9. Calculate each registered metric independently and produce per-case difference records.
10. Classify anomalies without changing registered artifacts.
11. Hash the complete reproducibility package and set the experiment record to collection complete.

Commands shall be inserted into this protocol and frozen before authorization. No command may invoke a provider, Local AI service, live agent, runtime orchestration, or network dependency.

## Canonical Comparison

Exact comparison includes all decision-relevant and descriptive pipeline artifacts. Only preregistered observational fields may be excluded:

- wall-clock timestamps generated by the measurement harness;
- elapsed-time values;
- host-specific environment fields;
- experiment repetition and run identifiers.

Excluded fields remain preserved in raw results. The exclusion list may not expand after authorization.

## Metrics and Analysis

Use only the metrics, calculations, outcome definitions, and descriptive statistical method registered in RP-001 Preregistration v1.1. Report no aggregate score, significance claim, improvement claim, or performance gate.

Latency reporting includes count, minimum, median, p95, maximum, mean, standard deviation, and coefficient of variation. It remains observational.

## Failure and Stop Rules

Stop further collection, preserve completed observations, and mark the event in the execution log if:

- a registered baseline or hash cannot be verified;
- the procedure would require a code, corpus, runtime, provider, or LOCKED change;
- unauthorized data or network/provider activity is detected;
- the harness is not executing the registered cases or order;
- authority, confidence, score, rank, vote, governance, or provider-identity decision semantics appear;
- continuing would overwrite or lose raw evidence.

An ordinary case failure or unexpected output is retained and does not itself stop the experiment unless integrity or safety is threatened.
