# CortexMesh Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

CortexMesh contributors, maintainers, reviewers, and participants commit to a respectful, inclusive, evidence-oriented community. Participation is open regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity or expression, experience level, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, caste, colour, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Expected Behaviour

- Be respectful, specific, and constructive.
- Critique ideas, evidence, code, and process rather than people.
- Distinguish observation from interpretation and authority.
- Acknowledge uncertainty, limitations, negative results, and mistakes.
- Preserve confidentiality, security boundaries, and research integrity.
- Accept reasonable moderation and review decisions.

## Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, threats, intimidation, stalking, or discriminatory conduct.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome attention.
- Personal attacks, insults, trolling, or sustained disruption.
- Publishing another person's private information without explicit permission.
- Public disclosure of security-sensitive information before coordinated remediation.
- Manipulating evidence, attribution, authorship, review, or governance records.
- Conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Scope

This Code applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions, research collaboration, governance communication, and public representation of CortexMesh. It also applies when someone officially represents the project.

## Reporting and Enforcement

Report conduct concerns privately to the repository owner through their GitHub profile or another private channel the owner has established. Do not include sensitive personal information in a public issue.

Maintainers will review reports in good faith, protect privacy where practical, avoid conflicts of interest, and respond proportionately. Possible actions include a private warning, content removal, temporary participation limits, rejection of a contribution, or permanent exclusion from project spaces.

Retaliation against a person who raises a good-faith concern is prohibited. Knowingly false or malicious reports may themselves result in corrective action.

This document governs community participation. It does not alter GG-02, create Board authority, or replace legal or emergency processes.
